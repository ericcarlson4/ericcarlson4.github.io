Rosewood is a typeface that brings back feelings of olden times. Based on the work of William Page, one of the 19th Century’s most important wood block typographers, Rosewood reminds the contemporary viewer of a time

when type was a decorative art form. Consisting of an outer and inner version of the typeface, Rosewood allows one to overlay text, much like the way text was printed before computers and printers.
